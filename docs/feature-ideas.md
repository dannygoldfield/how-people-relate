## HPR Cookbook System

Develop HPR audio through a cookbook metaphor.

Core units:
- Beds: environmental sound layers
- Gestures: small nearby physical events
- Recipes: reusable combinations of beds, gestures, stems, motion, and image treatment
- Taste tests: subjective ratings after auditioning

Goal:
Create a repeatable system that produces subtle, tactile, human-scale sound for portraits while preserving room for surprise.

# Creative Cookbook

Working idea:
The Cookbook is not an editor. It is a creative instrument.

Its purpose is not to automate creative decisions.
Its purpose is to create conditions where better creative decisions can emerge than the creator would have reached alone.

The goal is not efficiency.
The goal is productive surprise.

---

Robert Altman principle

Robert Altman said that directing actors is "not like extracting a tooth."

The best outcome is when an actor gives a performance unlike anything the director could have imagined.

The director's role is to create the conditions where those performances can emerge.

The Cookbook should work the same way.

---

Core Principle

Generate combinations that a human would be unlikely to choose, but likely to appreciate.

Not random.

Not deterministic.

Constrained surprise.

---

Creative philosophy

The software should learn the creator's taste, not replace it.

The creator always makes the final decision.

The software expands the creator's guess space.

---

Cookbook metaphor

Pantry
Library of ingredients.

Ingredients
Beds
Gestures
Music stems
Voice
Motion
Typography
Grain
Color treatments

Recipes
Reusable combinations of ingredients.

Taste Tests
Subjective ratings after real-world use.

Favorites
Recipes that consistently produce satisfying results.

---

HPR audio model

Episode = Bed + Gesture

Bed
Continuous environmental layer.

Gesture
One or two nearby physical events.

The goal is presence, not sound design.

---

Long-term vision

The Cookbook should not be limited to audio.

A recipe may eventually include:

- portrait treatment
- crop
- motion
- sound bed
- gestures
- music stem
- typography
- caption style
- export settings

A recipe becomes a reusable creative treatment.

---

Question

How can the Cookbook help creators discover combinations they would never have tried on their own while preserving complete creative agency?
